🧱 Foundation — Core Formulae
Definition: Average = Sum of terms / Total number of terms Basic Formulae: 1. Avg of n natural numbers = (n+1) / 2 2. Avg of n even numbers = n + 1 3. Avg of n odd numbers = n 4. Avg of n consecutive naturals = (First + Last) / 2 5. Avg sum of squares of first n = (n+1)(2n+1) / 6
⚠️If same value added/subtracted/multiplied/divided to ALL terms — average changes the SAME way
Operation on every termEffect on average
Add aAverage + a
Subtract aAverage − a
Multiply by aAverage × a
Divide by aAverage ÷ a
🧱 Foundation — Useful Formulae
Formula 1 — Combining two groups: Avg of x numbers = a, Avg of y numbers = b Combined avg = (xa + yb) / (x + y) Example: 10 numbers avg 15, 15 numbers avg 20 = (10×15 + 15×20) / 25 = 450/25 = 18
Formula 2 — Removed quantity: Avg of n quantities = x. One removed → avg = y Removed value = n(x − y) + y Example: Avg of 24 men + 1 woman = 35. Woman leaves → avg 34 Age of woman = 25(35−34) + 34 = 59 years
Formula 3 — Pass/Fail split: n students, overall avg = M Passed avg = P, Failed avg = F No. of failed = n(P − M) / (P − F) Example: 125 students, avg=29, passed=36, failed=11 Failed = 125(36−29)/(36−11) = 125×7/25 = 35
Formula 4 — Batsman innings: nth inning score = s, avg increases by t Average after n innings = s − t(n−1) Example: 44th inning score=86, avg increases by 1 Avg = 86 − 1(44−1) = 86 − 43 = 43
Question Type 1
🎯 Inclusion / Exclusion Model
👀
Recognition
▼
"average increases/decreases when one person added/removed"
"teacher included/excluded — average changes by..."
"n persons + 1 more — find the extra person's value"
🚩Mental flag: Count changes → total changes → find unknown
⚡
Shortcut
▼
When 1 person ADDED, avg increases by d (new total = n+1): New person's value = old avg + d × (n+1) When 1 person REMOVED, avg changes: Removed value = n(old avg − new avg) + new avg General shortcut: Change in total = Change in avg × total count
⚠️When adding: use n+1 as denominator, NOT n
🚨
Trap
▼
TrapCorrection
Using n instead of n+1 when addingTotal becomes n+1 after addition
Ignoring direction of changeIncrease in avg → added value > old avg
Mixing removed vs replaced formulaReplacement = two separate changes
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Avg age of 30 students = 15 yrs. Teacher included → avg increases by 1. Teacher's age?
Total now = 31
Teacher's age = 15 + 1×31 = 46
Answer: 46 years
Q2. Avg wt of 10 persons increases by 1.5 kg when one weighing 50 kg is replaced.
Weight increase = 1.5×10 = 15 kg
New person's weight = 50 + 15 = 65
Answer: 65 kg
Q3. Avg score in 10 innings = 45. Runs needed in 11th to increase avg by 5?
Required avg = 50
Score = 50×11 − 45×10 = 550−450 = 100
Answer: 100 runs
Q4. Avg age of 24 students + teacher = 16. Without teacher avg = 15. Teacher's age?
Teacher's age = 25(16−15) + 15 = 25+15
Answer: 40 years
Q5. Avg wt of 8 persons increases by 2.5 kg when new person replaces one weighing 65 kg.
New person = 65 + 2.5×8 = 65+20
Answer: 85 kg
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyAvg of 5 = 20. One number 30 removed. New avg? → 17.5
MediumAvg of 20 = 35. One number misread 85 as 58. Correct avg? → 36.35
HardAvg of 9 = 50. If one number doubled, avg becomes 55. Find the number. → 45
Question Type 2
🎯 Replacement Model
👀
Recognition
▼
"one student replaced by another — avg changes"
"a group leaves and new group joins"
"highest/lowest value replaced — new avg?"
⚡
Shortcut
▼
Single replacement: New value = Old value + (change in avg × n) Group replacement: New total = Old total − (leaving avg × count) + (joining avg × count) New avg = New total / n Key insight: Track TOTAL change, not individual
🚨
Trap
▼
TrapCorrection
n changes when group replacedn stays same if equal count replaced
Using avg change directlyAlways compute new total first
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Avg wt of 15 students = 20 kg. Student weighing 25 kg replaced → avg becomes 19 kg. New student's weight?
New total = 19×15 = 285
Old total = 20×15 = 300
New student = 285 − (300−25) = 285−275
Answer: 10 kg
Q2. Avg age of 40 students = 16. 10 students (avg 15) leave; 10 new (avg 17) join. New avg?
Old total = 640
New total = 640 − 150 + 170 = 660
New avg = 660/40
Answer: 16.5 years
Q3. Avg of 10 batsmen = 35. Highest 75 replaced by 25. New avg?
Change = 25−75 = −50
New avg = 35 + (−50/10) = 35−5
Answer: 30 runs
Q4. Avg monthly salary 20 employees = ₹1500. Manager included → avg +₹100. Manager's salary?
Manager = 1500 + 100×21 = 1500+2100
Answer: ₹3600
Q5. Avg age of 5 family members = 24. Youngest = 8 yrs. Avg just before youngest was born?
Total now = 120
Other 4 members at birth = 120−8−(4×8) = 120−8−32 = 80
Avg of 4 at that time = 80/4
Answer: 20 years
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyAvg of 6 = 30. One number 50 replaced by 30. New avg? → 26.67
Medium20 students avg 40. 5 leave (avg 35), 5 join (avg 45). New avg? → 41.25

Question Type 3
🎯 AP / Series Average Model
👀
Recognition
▼
"average of first n natural/even/odd numbers"
"average of multiples of n"
"average of consecutive numbers from a to b"
"average of series: a, a+d, a+2d..."
⚡
Shortcut
▼
AP Average = (First + Last) / 2 = Middle term (if odd count) Avg of first n naturals = (n+1)/2 Avg of first n even numbers = n+1 Avg of first n odd numbers = n Avg of first n multiples of k = k(n+1)/2 Consecutive a to b: Avg = (a+b)/2
⚠️For equal-spaced series: middle value = average (direct answer)
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Average of first 50 natural numbers.
Avg = (50+1)/2 = 51/2
Answer: 25.5
Q2. Average of all even numbers from 1 to 100.
Even numbers 2,4,...,100 → n=50
Avg of first n even = n+1 = 51
Answer: 51
Q3. Average of first 20 multiples of 7.
Multiples: 7,14,...,140
Avg = 7(20+1)/2 = 7×21/2 = 147/2
Answer: 73.5
Q4. Average of all odd numbers between 20 and 40.
Odd: 21,23,...,39
Avg = (21+39)/2
Answer: 30
Q5. Average of series 1, 3, 5, ..., 99.
First 50 odd numbers (n=50)
Avg of first n odd = n
Answer: 50
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyAvg of first 100 natural numbers → 50.5
MediumAvg of all multiples of 3 from 3 to 99 → 51
HardAvg of all numbers divisible by 6 between 100 and 200 → 150
Question Type 4
🎯 Wrongly Entered Data Model
👀
Recognition
▼
"misread as / wrongly written as"
"it was later found that..."
"correct average / correct sum"
⚡
Shortcut
▼
Correct sum = Wrong sum − Wrong value + Correct value Correct avg = Wrong avg + (Correct − Wrong) / n If each term multiplied by k: New avg = Old avg × k
⚠️If multiplied/divided by same number — just multiply/divide average. No individual values needed.
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Avg of 20 numbers = 35. One number 85 was misread as 58. Correct avg?
Correct avg = 35 + (85−58)/20 = 35 + 27/20 = 35 + 1.35
Answer: 36.35
Q2. Avg marks of 100 students = 40. Score of 53 misread as 83. Correct avg?
Correct avg = 40 + (53−83)/100 = 40 − 0.3
Answer: 39.7
Q3. Avg of 5 observations = 60. One observation 75 should be 25. Correct avg?
Correct avg = 60 + (25−75)/5 = 60 − 10
Answer: 50
Q4. Avg age of 30 students = 14 yr 4 mo. One student's age misread 16 instead of 14. Correct avg?
Correction = (14−16)/30 = −2/30 yr = −0.8 months
Correct avg = 14 yr 4 mo − 0.8 mo
Answer: 14 yr 3 mo 8 days (approx)
Q5. Avg of 10 numbers = 20. Each number multiplied by 2. New avg?
Multiply all → avg multiplied same way
New avg = 20 × 2
Answer: 40
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyAvg of 15 = 50. Number 75 misread as 57. Correct avg? → 51.2
MediumAvg of 25 = 60. Two numbers 37&28 misread as 73&82. Correct avg? → 56.4
HardAvg of n = 40. One number misread +18. Avg becomes 41. Find n. → 18
Question Type 5
🎯 Average Speed Model
👀
Recognition
▼
"travels to and fro at different speeds"
"covers equal/unequal distances at different speeds"
"find average speed for entire journey"
⚡
Shortcut
▼
Two equal distances, speeds a and b: Avg speed = 2ab / (a + b) ← Harmonic Mean Three equal distances, speeds a, b, c: Avg speed = 3abc / (ab + bc + ca) Unequal distances: Avg speed = Total distance / Total time
⚠️Average speed ≠ (a+b)/2 for equal distances — this is the #1 trap in speed problems
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. A→B at 40 km/h, returns at 60 km/h. Average speed?
Equal distances → Avg = 2×40×60/(40+60) = 4800/100
Answer: 48 km/h
Q2. Car covers 100 km each at 50, 40, 25 km/h. Average speed?
Total distance = 300 km
Total time = 100/50 + 100/40 + 100/25 = 2+2.5+4 = 8.5 hrs
Avg = 300/8.5
Answer: 35.29 km/h
Q3. Three equal distances at 10, 15, 30 km/h.
Avg = 3×10×15×30 / (150+450+300) = 13500/900
Answer: 15 km/h
Q4. Train at 30 km/h for 2 hrs, then 45 km/h for 3 hrs. Average speed?
Total dist = 60+135 = 195 km
Total time = 5 hrs
Avg = 195/5
Answer: 39 km/h
Q5. Home→office at 20 km/h, return at 30 km/h. Total time = 5 hrs. Distance?
Let distance = d
d/20 + d/30 = 5 → (3d+2d)/60 = 5 → 5d = 300
Answer: 60 km
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyTravel at 30, return at 60 km/h. Avg speed? → 40 km/h
Medium4 equal distances at 10, 20, 30, 60 km/h. Avg speed? → 20 km/h
HardA:B:C distance ratio 1:2:3, speeds 10:20:30 km/h. Avg speed? → 18 km/h
Question Type 6
🎯 Cricket / Batting Average
👀
Recognition
▼
"batsman's average increases/decreases after nth inning"
"runs needed to reach target average"
"bowler's average improves by x after y wickets"
⚡
Shortcut
▼
Batsman — avg after nth inning: Avg = s − t(n−1) where s = score in nth inning, t = increase in avg Bowler formula: Avg = total runs / total wickets New avg = (old total + new runs) / (old + new wkts) Runs needed for target avg A after n innings: = current avg × (n−1) + increase × n
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Batsman avg = 87 in 17 innings. Runs needed in 18th to increase avg by 3?
Required avg = 90
Runs = 90×18 − 87×17 = 1620−1479
Answer: 141 runs
Q2. Cricketer's 11th inning = 100 runs, avg increases by 5. New avg?
Avg = s − t(n−1) = 100 − 5(11−1) = 100−50
Answer: 50 runs
Q3. Bowler avg = 12.4. Takes 5 wkts for 26 runs, avg decreases by 0.4. Total wickets before?
New avg = 12.0
(12.4n + 26)/(n+5) = 12.0
12.4n + 26 = 12n + 60
0.4n = 34 → n = 85
Answer: 85 wickets
Q4. Batsman in 12th inning scores 90, avg increases by 5. Avg after 12th?
Avg = 90 − 5(12−1) = 90−55
Answer: 35 runs
Q5. Bowler avg = 24.85. Takes 5 wkts for 52 runs, avg improves by 0.85. Total wickets now?
New avg = 24.0
(24.85n+52)/(n+5) = 24.0
0.85n = 68 → n=80
Total now = 80+5
Answer: 85 wickets
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyBatsman avg 50 in 10 innings. Runs for 11th to keep avg 50? → 50
MediumAvg after 20 = 40. After 21st inning avg = 41. Score in 21st? → 61
HardBowler: 30 wkts, avg 20. Takes 6 wkts for 18 runs. New avg? → 19.75
Question Type 7
🎯 Weighted Average (Alligation)
👀
Recognition
▼
"two groups, find ratio from combined avg"
"combined average given, find counts"
"mixture of two averages"
⚡
Shortcut — Alligation
▼
Group A avg = a, Group B avg = b, Combined = c a b \ / c (combined) / \ (b−c) (c−a) Ratio A:B = (b−c) : (c−a) Weighted avg formula: = (n1×a1 + n2×a2 + ...) / (n1 + n2 + ...)
📜
PYQ Style
▼
Q1. Boys avg = 60, Girls avg = 70, Class avg = 64. Ratio boys:girls?
Alligation: (70−64):(64−60) = 6:4
Answer: 3:2
Q2. 120 students avg 13. Boys avg 14, girls avg 12. Find boys and girls.
Ratio boys:girls = (13−12):(14−13) = 1:1
Boys = Girls = 120/2
Answer: 60 boys, 60 girls
Q3. 20 kg rice (10% impurity) + 30 kg (5% impurity). Combined impurity %?
Total impurity = 20×0.10 + 30×0.05 = 2+1.5 = 3.5 kg
Total = 50 kg → 3.5/50×100
Answer: 7%
Q4. Factory avg salary = ₹8000. 7 technicians avg ₹12000, rest avg ₹6000. Total employees?
Alligation: non-tech:tech = (12000−8000):(8000−6000) = 4000:2000 = 2:1
Non-tech = 2×7 = 14
Total = 14+7
Answer: 21 employees
Q5. Section A: 40 students avg 75. Section B: 60 students avg 80. Combined avg?
(40×75 + 60×80)/(100) = (3000+4800)/100
Answer: 78
🏆
Mastery
▼
EasyGroup A: 10 people avg 20. Group B: 20 people avg 30. Combined avg? → 26.67
MediumMilk at ₹20/L and ₹30/L mixed; cost = ₹24/L. Ratio? → 3:2
HardThree sections: 30(avg 70), 40(avg 80), 50(avg 60). Overall avg? → 69.17
🧮 Ages — Concept & Properties
📘 Key Terms & Properties
TermMeaningExample
Hence / LaterAge in FUTURE"5 years hence" = 5 yrs from now
Ago / ThenceAge in PAST"3 years ago" = 3 yrs before now
PresentCurrent ageNo time shift
Property 1 — Avg shifts with time: Present avg = x yrs n years AGO → avg was (x − n) n years LATER → avg will be (x + n) Property 2 — Age difference is CONSTANT: Age difference between two persons never changes → Use this as the KEY equation in ratio problems Property 3 — Child born in family: Include child in count from birth onwards Before birth: old count; After birth: new count Property 4 — Ratio problems: Let future/past ages as kx, form equations Use age difference = constant to solve
⚠️Age difference NEVER changes — this is the most powerful tool in ages problems
📝 Ages — 8 Solved Examples (All Types)
Ex1 [Ratio + Future] 4 yrs ago A is 4 yrs younger than B. 6 yrs hence ratio A:B = 16:17. Sum of ages 4 yrs ago?
Let ages 6 yrs hence: A=16x, B=17x
Age difference = 17x−16x = x = 4 (constant, given 4 yrs ago A is 4 yrs younger)
Ages 6 yrs hence: A=64, B=68
Ages 4 yrs ago: A=64−10=54, B=68−10=58
Sum = 54+58
Answer: 112 years
Ex2 [Child Born] Family of 3, avg age 4 yrs ago = 20. Child born in this period. Avg 3 yrs hence = 21.5. Child's present age?
Sum including child 3 yrs hence = 21.5×4 = 86
Sum excluding child 3 yrs hence = (20+4+3)×3 = 81
Child's age 3 yrs hence = 86−81 = 5
Child's present age = 5−3
Answer: 2 years
Ex3 [Two Unknowns] Sum of Hari & Mohan's ages = double their difference. 4 yrs ago ratio = 3:2. Ratio after 12 yrs?
Let Hari=x, Mohan=y
x+y = 2(x−y) → x = 3y ...(i)
4 yrs ago: (x−4+y−4) = (3/2){(x−4)−(y−4)}
2x+2y−16 = 3x−3y → 5y−x = 16 ...(ii)
From (i): 5y−3y = 16 → 2y = 16 → y=8, x=24
After 12 yrs: Hari=36, Mohan=20
Ratio = 36:20
Answer: 9:5
Ex4 [Father-Children] Geeta 5 yrs older than Mahesh. Father = 2×(sum of their ages). At Mahesh's birth, father = 8×Geeta's age.
Let Mahesh=x, Geeta=x+5, Father=2(x+x+5)=4x+10
At Mahesh's birth: Geeta=5, Father=4x+10−x=3x+10
3x+10 = 8×5 = 40 → 3x=30 → x=10
Answer: Mahesh=10, Geeta=15, Father=50
Ex5 [Innings Average] Batsman scores 34, 36, 38, 40, 42 in five innings. Average?
Sum = 34+36+38+40+42 = 190
Avg = 190/5
Answer: 38 runs
Ex6 [Remaining Average] Avg of 10 quantities = 12. Avg of 6 of them = 8. Avg of remaining 4?
Total of 10 = 120
Total of 6 = 48
Remaining sum = 72
Avg = 72/4
Answer: 18
Ex7 [Height Replacement] Avg height 50 students = 152 cm. 10 (avg 148) leave; 10 new (avg 150) join. New avg?
Total = 7600
After change = 7600 − 1480 + 1500 = 7620
New avg = 7620/50
Answer: 152.4 cm
Ex8 [Salary + Alligation] Office avg salary = ₹120. Officers avg = ₹460, non-officers = ₹110. Officers = 15. Find non-officers.
Alligation ratio non-officers:officers = (460−120):(120−110) = 340:10 = 34:1
Non-officers = 15×34
Answer: 510
🚨 Master Trap Sheet
Every item = wrong answer in SSC/RRB/Banking papers
01Average speed for equal distances — NOT (a+b)/2, use 2ab/(a+b)
02Adding 1 person to n-person group — denominator becomes n+1, not n
03Multiply each term by k — avg multiplied by k (no individual values needed)
04Age difference — ALWAYS constant at any point of time
05Child born — count — include child in count; before birth use old count
06"n years ago" average — subtract n from current avg (not multiply)
07Pass/fail formula — n(P−M)/(P−F), NOT n(M−F)/(P−F)
08Batsman formula — s − t(n−1), NOT s − t(n)
09Removed quantity formula — n = ORIGINAL count (including removed person)
10Weighted avg ratio — alligation gives ratio, not individual counts directly
11Wrong data correction — Correct avg = Wrong avg + (correct−wrong)/n
12Avg of n naturals — (n+1)/2, NOT n/2
13Avg of n even numbers — n+1, NOT n
14Avg of n odd numbers — n, NOT n+1
15Three equal distances — use 3abc/(ab+bc+ca), NOT arithmetic mean of speeds
