🧱 FOUNDATION

F1. The Three Prices

CP  = Cost Price     → what you PAY to get the article
SP  = Selling Price  → what you SELL it for
MP  = Marked Price   → label/printed price (before discount)

CP → add markup → MP → subtract discount → SP

Key relationships:

Profit  = SP − CP   (when SP > CP)
Loss    = CP − SP   (when CP > SP)
Discount = MP − SP  (always on MP)

F2. Core Formulas

Profit%   = (Profit / CP) × 100
Loss%     = (Loss / CP) × 100
Discount% = (Discount / MP) × 100

SP = CP × (100 + Profit%) / 100
SP = CP × (100 − Loss%) / 100

CP = SP × 100 / (100 + Profit%)
CP = SP × 100 / (100 − Loss%)

SP = MP × (100 − Discount%) / 100
MP = SP × 100 / (100 − Discount%)

⚠️ Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS calculated on CP — unless explicitly stated otherwise
⚠️ Discount% is ALWAYS calculated on MP — never on CP or SP

F3. Multiplier Method (fastest)

Profit of 20%  → multiply CP by 1.20
Loss of 20%    → multiply CP by 0.80
Discount of 20% → multiply MP by 0.80
Markup of 30%  → multiply CP by 1.30

Chain: CP → MP → SP

If markup = 30% and discount = 20%:
SP = CP × 1.30 × 0.80 = CP × 1.04
→ Net profit = 4%

🎯 TYPE 1 — BASIC CP ↔ SP ↔ PROFIT/LOSS

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "bought for...sold for...profit/loss is?"
- "sells at 14% profit for ₹2850, find CP"
- "SP of 12 articles = CP of 15 articles, find gain%"
- "bought at rate of X per rupee, sold at rate of Y per rupee"

⚡ Shortcut

Finding CP from SP and Profit%

CP = SP × 100 / (100 + Profit%)
CP = SP × 100 / (100 − Loss%)

SP of x articles = CP of y articles

If CP of x = SP of y → Profit% = (x−y)/y × 100
If SP of x = CP of y → Profit% = (y−x)/x × 100
                                [or Loss% if negative]

Buying at rate of m per ₹1, selling at n per ₹1

CP of 1 article = 1/m
SP of 1 article = 1/n
Profit% = (m−n)/n × 100   [if n < m → profit]
Loss%   = (n−m)/m × 100   [if n > m → loss]

Two different buying rates, one selling rate

Use LCM of the two "per rupee" numbers to equalise quantity
Then: Total CP vs Total SP → find Profit/Loss%

🚨 Trap

Trap Correction
Profit% on SP Question says "on CP unless stated" — check wording
SP of 12 = CP of 15 → 12/15 = loss WRONG — more CP articles for same money = gain. Apply formula: (15−12)/12 × 100 = 25% profit
Buying at 11 for ₹10 and 9 for ₹10 → rate 10 = no profit Must take LCM and compute actual CP vs SP
"Sold at cost price" = no profit/loss Only true for honest dealers — check for false weight

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Bicycle sold for ₹2850 at 14% profit. Find CP. Then find SP at 8% profit.

CP = 2850 × 100/114 = ₹2500
SP at 8% = 2500 × 108/100 = ₹2700

Q2. SP of 12 articles = CP of 15 articles. Find gain%.

Gain% = (15−12)/12 × 100 = 3/12 × 100 = 25%

Q3. Article: profit 20% on CP. Find profit% on SP.

Let CP = 100, Profit = 20, SP = 120
Profit% on SP = 20/120 × 100 = 16⅔%

Q4. Fruit seller: buys 11 for ₹10 and 9 for ₹10. Sells all at ₹1 each. Gain/Loss%?

LCM(11,9) = 99 fruits of each type
CP = (10/11)×99 + (10/9)×99 = 90 + 110 = ₹200
SP = 198 × 1 = ₹198
Loss% = (200−198)/200 × 100 = 1%

Q5. Buys at 5 per rupee, same number at 4 per rupee. Mixes and sells at 9 for ₹2. Loss = ₹3. Total articles?

Per 10 articles: CP = 1/5×5 + 1/4×5 = 1+1.25 = ₹9/4
SP of 10 = (2/9)×10 = ₹20/9
Loss per 10 = 9/4 − 20/9 = (81−80)/36 = ₹1/36
Loss = ₹3 → articles = 10 × 3 × 36 = 1080

🏆 Mastery

Easy: CP = ₹120, sells at 20% profit. Find SP.
Medium: SP of 20 articles = CP of 16 articles. Find loss%.
Hard: A person buys 5 articles for ₹4 and sells 4 articles for ₹5. Find profit%.

(Answers: ₹144 | 20% loss | CP per article = 4/5; SP per article = 5/4; Profit% = (5/4−4/5)/(4/5)×100 = (9/20)/(4/5)×100 = 56.25%)

🎯 TYPE 2 — MARKED PRICE & DISCOUNT

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "marked price...discount...selling price"
- "bought at X% discount on labelled price, sold at Y% profit on labelled price"
- "after giving 20% discount, shopkeeper still makes 20% profit — find MP"
- "reduction of x% enables buying y more for ₹Z"

⚡ Shortcut

MP → SP → CP chain

SP = MP × (100 − d%) / 100
CP = SP × 100 / (100 + p%)

Net: SP = CP × (100+p%) / 100 = MP × (100−d%) / 100
→ CP/MP = (100−d%) / (100+p%)

Find MP given SP and discount%

MP = SP × 100 / (100 − discount%)

Markup% needed to allow x% discount and still make y% profit

(100 + markup%) × (100 − discount%) / 100 = (100 + profit%)
→ (100 + markup%) = (100 + profit%) × 100 / (100 − discount%)

"Price reduction → buy more" type

Savings = reduction% × total money
Extra articles bought at NEW price
New price per unit = Savings / extra articles
Old price = New price × 100 / (100 − reduction%)

🚨 Trap

Trap Correction
Discount on CP Discount is ALWAYS on MP
Profit on SP Profit is ALWAYS on CP (unless stated)
"20% profit on labelled price" = 20% profit on CP WRONG — profit on CP vs profit on MP are different
MP = CP + profit% Only if no discount is given and SP = MP
20% discount + 20% profit → net = 0 WRONG — apply multipliers: ×0.8×1.2 = ×0.96 → actual net

⚠️ Always work backward: SP known → find MP or CP using their respective formulas

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. CP = ₹300, markup = 30%. Find MP.

MP = 300 × 1.30 = ₹390

Q2. Ashish buys at 20% discount on labelled price. Sells at 30% profit on labelled price. Profit% on his buying price?

Let labelled price = 100
CP (his) = 80, SP (his) = 130
Profit = 130 − 80 = 50
Profit% = 50/80 × 100 = 62.5%

Q3. Sold for ₹400 after 20% discount. Made 20% profit on CP. If no discount given, what profit%?

MP = 400 × 100/80 = ₹500
CP = 400 × 100/120 = ₹1000/3
If sold at MP: Profit% = (500 − 1000/3)/(1000/3) × 100
= (1500−1000)/3 ÷ (1000/3) × 100
= (500/3)/(1000/3) × 100 = 50%

Q4. 20% reduction in mango price → can buy 12 more for ₹15. Price of 16 mangoes before reduction?

Savings = 20% of 15 = ₹3
₹3 buys 12 mangoes at new price → new price = 3/12 = ₹0.25/mango
Old price = 0.25 × 100/80 = ₹0.3125 = 31.25 paise
16 mangoes at old price = 16 × 31.25/100 = ₹5

Q5. Garment company: 15% discount for wholesale. Hemant buys for ₹8500 after discount. Sells at 10% profit on original company price. Find SP.

Original (MP) = 8500 × 100/85 = ₹10,000
SP = 10000 × 1.10 = ₹11,000

🏆 Mastery

Easy: MP = ₹500. Discount 20%. SP = ?
Medium: After 25% discount, profit is 12.5%. What is markup%?
Hard: A dealer marks 40% above CP and allows 25% discount. On a further bargain of ₹10, he makes only 5% profit. Find CP.

(Answers: ₹400 | (1+m%)×0.75=1.125 → m% = 50% | CP×1.40×0.75 = CP×1.05; extra ₹10 brings from 5% to actual; CP×1.05−10 = CP×1.05_adj; solve → ₹200)

🎯 TYPE 3 — SUCCESSIVE DISCOUNTS

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "two successive discounts of x% and y%"
- "first discount 30%, then another 20%"
- "buy 3 get 1 free"
- "equivalent/net discount"

⚡ Shortcut

Two successive discounts a% and b%

Net discount = a + b − (ab/100) %

Equivalently: SP = MP × (1−a/100) × (1−b/100)

Three successive discounts

Apply pairwise:
First: net₁ = a + b − ab/100
Then: net = net₁ + c − (net₁ × c)/100

Buy x get y free → discount%

Discount% = y/(x+y) × 100

Reason: (x+y) articles sold at CP of x articles
SP of (x+y) = x × (CP per article)

🚨 Trap

Trap Correction
50% + 50% = 100% discount Net = 50+50−25 = 75% (not 100%)
30% + 20% = 50% Net = 30+20−6 = 44%
Successive discounts are commutative Yes — order doesn't matter for net effect
"Buy 3 get 1 free" → 25% discount Discount% = 1/(3+1) × 100 = 25% ✓
"Buy 4 get 1 free" → 25% discount Discount% = 1/(4+1) × 100 = 20% (not 25%)

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Two successive discounts 50% and 50%. Net discount?

= 50 + 50 − (50×50/100) = 100 − 25 = 75%

Q2. Successive discounts 20% and 10%. Net discount?

= 20 + 10 − (20×10/100) = 30 − 2 = 28%

Q3. Buy 2 get 1 free. Effective discount%?

= 1/(2+1) × 100 = 33.33%

Q4. Three successive discounts: 20%, 10%, 5%. Net?

Step 1: 20+10−2 = 28%
Step 2: 28+5−(28×5/100) = 33−1.4 = 31.6%

Q5. MP = ₹8400. Sales tax reduced from 3½% to 3⅓%. Difference in amount paid?

Difference in rate = 3½ − 3⅓ = 7/2 − 10/3 = 21/6 − 20/6 = 1/6%
Amount saved = (1/6)% of 8400 = 8400/600 = ₹14

🏆 Mastery

Easy: Net discount for successive 25% and 20%?
Medium: MP = ₹1000. First 20% discount then 10% off the reduced price. Final SP?
Hard: A shopkeeper allows 30% discount and still makes 10.5% profit. If he allows no discount, what % profit?

(Answers: 40% | ₹720 | SP=MP×0.7; CP=SP/1.105; if SP=MP: profit=(MP−CP)/CP×100 = (0.3×MP/CP)×100 → 57.86%)

🎯 TYPE 4 — SAME SP, EQUAL PROFIT% & LOSS%

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "sold at same price...one at x% profit...other at x% loss"
- "two articles each sold for ₹X..."
- "percentage profit = percentage loss"

⚡ Shortcut

Same SP, same profit% and loss%

ALWAYS a net loss
Loss% = (Common %)² / 100 = (P/10)²%

Absolute loss = 2P²x / (100² − P²)
where x = SP of each article, P = common %

Same CP, same profit% and loss%

No gain, no loss (they cancel perfectly)

🚨 Trap

Trap Correction
Same SP, equal P% and L% → break even ALWAYS a loss — the loss article's CP is higher
Same CP, equal P% and L% → loss No — break even when CP is same
Loss% = Common% WRONG — Loss% = (Common%)²/100
10% profit + 10% loss → 1% loss Correct formula: (10)²/100 = 1% loss ✓

⚠️ "Same SP" = always loss. "Same CP" = always break even.

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Two cars each sold for ₹1000. One at 25% profit, other at 25% loss. Net%?

Loss% = (25)²/100 = 625/100 = 6.25%

Q2. Two articles each sold at ₹300. One at 20% profit, one at 20% loss. Absolute loss?

Loss = 2×(20²)×300 / (100²−20²)
= 2×400×300 / (10000−400)
= 240000/9600 = ₹25

Q3. Two cycles sold for ₹1710 total. CP of 1st = SP of 2nd. 1st sold at 10% loss, 2nd at 25% gain. Total gain/loss?

Let CP of 1st = 100 (= SP of 2nd)
SP of 1st = 90 (10% loss)
CP of 2nd = 100×100/125 = 80 (since SP=100 at 25% gain)
Total CP = 100+80 = 180; Total SP = 90+100 = 190
CP:SP = 18:19
Profit = (1/19) × 1710 = ₹90

🏆 Mastery

Easy: Two items each sold at ₹500. One at 10% profit, one at 10% loss. Net profit/loss?
Medium: Two items each sold at ₹480. One at 20% profit, one at 20% loss. Find total CP and total loss.
Hard: Two articles sold for ₹990 each. One at 10% profit, one at 10% loss. Total SP = ₹1980. How much money was lost?

(Answers: 1% loss = ₹10 total | CP₁=400,CP₂=600,Total=1000,Loss=40 | Loss=2×100×990/(10000−100)=1980/99=₹20)

🎯 TYPE 5 — DISHONEST DEALER (FALSE WEIGHT)

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "sells at cost price but uses false weight"
- "uses 900g instead of 1kg"
- "cheats by x grams per kg"
- "professes to sell at CP"

⚡ Shortcut

Gain% = Error / (True value − Error) × 100
      = (True weight − False weight) / False weight × 100

Where:
Error = True weight − False weight

Memory hook: Gain is on the FALSE weight (what customer actually gets)

🚨 Trap

Trap Correction
Gain% = Error/True weight × 100 WRONG — denominator is TRUE − ERROR (= false weight)
960g instead of 1000g → gain = 40/1000 × 100 = 4% WRONG — gain = 40/960 × 100 = 4.16%
Dealer sells AT a profit AND uses false weight Compound: apply both multipliers
"Uses 800g instead of 1kg" → gain% = 20% Gain = 200/800 × 100 = 25%

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Shopkeeper uses 960g instead of 1kg, sells at CP. Profit%?

Gain% = (1000−960)/960 × 100 = 40/960 × 100 = 4 1/6%

Q2. Trader uses 800g instead of 1000g AND sells at 10% profit. Overall gain%?

Gain from false weight = 200/800 × 100 = 25%
Combined: (1.25 × 1.10 − 1) × 100 = 37.5%

Q3. A dishonest dealer claims to sell at CP but gains 25%. What weight does he use per kg?

25 = Error/(1000−Error) × 100
25(1000−Error) = 100×Error
25000 = 125×Error
Error = 200g → uses 800g per kg

🏆 Mastery

Easy: Dealer uses 900g instead of 1kg. Profit%?
Medium: A shopkeeper cheats by 10% while buying and 10% while selling. Net profit%?
Hard: A dealer uses false weight and gives 10% discount on MP. Still makes 20% profit on actual CP. What is his markup% and what weight per kg does he use?

(Answers: 11.11% | Buys 1100g for price of 1000g AND sells 900g for price of 1000g → net = 1.1×(10000/9000)=1.222 → 22.2% | requires two unknowns, set up simultaneous)

🎯 TYPE 6 — SP OF x ARTICLES = CP OF y ARTICLES

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "SP of 12 = CP of 15"
- "CP of x articles = SP of y articles"
- "by selling 80 articles he gains CP of 20 articles"

⚡ Shortcut

CASE A: SP of x = CP of y (y > x → profit)
Profit% = (y − x) / x × 100

CASE B: CP of x = SP of y (x > y → profit)
Profit% = (x − y) / y × 100

CASE C: "Gains CP of z articles by selling n articles"
→ means: Profit = z × (CP per article)
→ SP of n = CP of n + CP of z = CP of (n+z)
→ Apply Case B with y=n, x=n+z

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. SP of 12 = CP of 15. Gain%?

Profit% = (15−12)/12 × 100 = 25%

Q2. CP of 20 = SP of 25. Loss%?

Loss% = (25−20)/25 × 100 = 20%

Q3. By selling 80 articles, gain = CP of 20 articles. Gain%?

SP of 80 = CP of 80 + CP of 20 = CP of 100
Profit% = (100−80)/80 × 100 = 25%

🏆 Mastery

Easy: SP of 10 articles = CP of 12 articles. Profit%?
Medium: SP of 16 articles = CP of 12 articles. Loss%?
Hard: By selling 56 articles a man gains the CP of 14 articles. Gain%?

(Answers: 20% | Loss=(16−12)/16×100=25% | SP of 56 = CP of 70; gain% = 14/56×100 = 25%)

🎯 TYPE 7 — "HAD HE SOLD FOR ₹X MORE" TYPE

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "had he sold for ₹X more, he would have gained Y%"
- "sold at 10% loss; if sold for ₹108 more, would get 10% profit"
- "difference between two SPs = ₹X"

⚡ Shortcut

Set up two SP equations:
SP₁ = CP × (100 ± p₁%) / 100
SP₂ = CP × (100 ± p₂%) / 100

SP₂ − SP₁ = given amount

Solve for CP:
CP × [(100±p₂) − (100±p₁)] / 100 = difference
CP = difference × 100 / (p₂ − p₁)   [if both same side]
CP = difference × 100 / (p₁ + p₂)   [one profit, one loss]

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Book sold at 10% loss. If sold for ₹108 more, 10% profit. Find CP.

SP₁ = 0.9 × CP
SP₂ = 1.1 × CP
SP₂ − SP₁ = 0.2 × CP = 108
CP = 108/0.2 = ₹540

Q2. Article sold at 5% loss. If sold for ₹96 more, 15% profit. Find CP.

0.2 × CP = 96 [same logic]
Wait: SP₂−SP₁ = (1.15−0.95)×CP = 0.20×CP = 96
CP = ₹480

Q3. Man sells at 8% profit. Had he sold for ₹150 more, profit would be 14%. Find CP.

(1.14−1.08) × CP = 150
0.06 × CP = 150
CP = ₹2500

🏆 Mastery

Easy: Sold at 5% loss; if sold for ₹20 more, would break even. Find CP.
Medium: Sold at 10% profit; if sold for ₹100 less, loss = 5%. Find CP.
Hard: Sold for ₹756 at 8% profit. If sold at 4% loss, what would SP be?

(Answers: ₹400 | 0.15×CP=100; CP=₹667 | CP=700; SP at 4% loss=700×0.96=₹672)

🎯 TYPE 8 — MIXED/PARTIAL SELLING (FRACTIONS)

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "sells 1/3 at loss of 20% and 2/5 at gain of 25%...remaining at what price for overall 10% profit?"
- "buys land for ₹X...sells parts at different P/L..."
- "two traders calculate profit on buying and selling price respectively"

⚡ Shortcut

Step 1: Find required total SP for overall profit/loss
  Total SP target = CP × (100 + overall%) / 100

Step 2: Find SP of each sold portion
  SP of fraction = fraction × CP × (100 ± their P or L%) / 100

Step 3: Remaining SP = Total target − sum of sold portions

Profit on CP vs Profit on SP (two traders)

Trader A: profit% on CP → straightforward
Trader B: claims same profit% but calculates on SP
  SP × profit% / 100 = profit amount
  CP = SP − profit = SP(1 − profit%/100)

Difference in actual profits → set up ratio → find SP

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Land ₹3,60,000. Sells 1/3 at 20% loss, 2/5 at 25% gain. Rest for overall 10% profit. Find remaining SP.

Total SP needed = 360000 × 1.10 = ₹396000
SP of 1/3 = (1/3)×360000×0.80 = ₹96000
SP of 2/5 = (2/5)×360000×1.25 = ₹180000
Remaining SP = 396000−96000−180000 = ₹120000

Q2. Two traders, same SP. Both claim 20% profit. One calculates on CP, other on SP. Actual profit difference = ₹85. Find SP.

Trader A (on CP): Let CP=100, SP=120; profit=₹20
Trader B (on SP): SP=120; profit=20%×120=₹24; CP=96
Actual profit B = 24, profit A = 20; difference = ₹4
When difference = ₹4, SP = ₹120
When difference = ₹85: SP = 120×85/4 = ₹2550

Q3. Man sells two cycles for ₹1710 total. 1st at 10% loss, 2nd at 25% gain. CP₁ = SP₂. Total gain?

[See Type 4, Q3 — same problem]
Profit = ₹90

🏆 Mastery

Easy: Buys 3 items for ₹100. Sells 2 at 10% profit, 1 at 10% loss. Overall profit/loss?
Medium: Sells ½ of goods at 20% profit and ½ at 20% loss. Overall?
Hard: Sells 40% at 25% profit, 30% at 10% loss, rest at 15% profit. Overall profit%?

(Answers: 2×1.1+1×0.9=3.1 SP vs 3 CP → gain 3.33% | exactly 4% loss (same SP rule) | 0.4×25−0.3×10+0.3×15 = 10−3+4.5 = 11.5%)

🎯 TYPE 9 — PROFIT ON CP vs PROFIT ON SP

👀 Recognition

Trigger words:
- "profit of 20% on cost price" vs "profit of 20% on selling price"
- "find profit% when calculated on selling price"
- "one trader calculates on buying price, another on selling price"

⚡ Shortcut

Profit% on CP = p%
→ Profit% on SP = p/(100+p) × 100

Profit% on SP = q%
→ Profit% on CP = q/(100−q) × 100

📜 PYQ Style

Q1. Profit 20% on CP. Find profit% on SP.

Profit% on SP = 20/(100+20) × 100 = 20/120 × 100 = 16⅔%

Q2. Profit 25% on SP. Find profit% on CP.

Profit% on CP = 25/(100−25) × 100 = 25/75 × 100 = 33⅓%

🔧 TOOLS

T1. Master Formula Sheet

Scenario Formula
SP from CP + profit% CP × (100+p)/100
CP from SP + profit% SP × 100/(100+p)
CP from SP + loss% SP × 100/(100−l)
MP from SP + discount% SP × 100/(100−d)
SP from MP + discount% MP × (100−d)/100
Two successive discounts a%, b% Net = a+b−ab/100
Same SP, same P% and L% Loss = P²/100%
False weight gain (True−False)/False × 100
SP x = CP y → profit (y−x)/x × 100
"₹X more → extra profit" CP = X × 100/(p₂+p₁)
Buy x get y free → discount y/(x+y) × 100
Profit on CP p% → profit on SP p/(100+p) × 100

T2. Multiplier Quick Reference

Operation Multiplier
Profit 10% ×1.10
Profit 20% ×1.20
Profit 25% ×1.25 = ×5/4
Profit 33⅓% ×4/3
Loss 10% ×0.90
Loss 20% ×0.80
Loss 25% ×0.75 = ×3/4
Discount 20% ×0.80

⚠️ MASTER TRAP SHEET

Every item here has caused wrong answers in SSC/Banking/CAT papers

  1. Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS on CP — unless question says "on SP"
  2. Discount% is ALWAYS on MP — never on CP or SP
  3. Same SP, same P% and L% → ALWAYS a loss of P²/100% (not break even)
  4. Same CP, same P% and L% → ALWAYS break even (not a loss)
  5. 50% + 50% successive discount ≠ 100% — net = 75%
  6. 30% + 20% ≠ 50% — net = 44% (must subtract cross term)
  7. False weight: gain% = Error/False weight × 100 (not Error/True weight)
  8. SP of 12 = CP of 15 → 25% profit (more money per article than you pay)
  9. CP of 15 = SP of 12 → 25% loss (getting less money per article)
  10. "₹X more" problems: CP = X × 100 / (sum of %) when one profit one loss
  11. Discount on labelled price ≠ profit on labelled price — these use different bases
  12. "Buy 4 get 1 free" → discount = 1/5 × 100 = 20% (not 25%)
  13. "Buy 3 get 1 free" → discount = 1/4 × 100 = 25% ✓
  14. Two traders, same SP, both 20% profit on different bases → different CPs and actual profits
  15. Net effect: markup then discount → SP = CP × (1+m%) × (1−d%) — always multiply, never add/subtract%
  16. Profit on SP → Profit on CP: divide by (100−p%), not add the same %
  17. False weight + extra profit: use compound multiplier, not add the two gains
  18. "Remaining part" problems: total SP target − sum of parts already sold = remaining SP
  19. CP of first = SP of second (linked items): set up ratio table, don't assume equal CPs
  20. Profit% on SP will always be LESS than profit% on CP for the same transaction
