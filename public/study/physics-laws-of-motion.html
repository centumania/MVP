🌐The Big Picture

First describe motion (kinematics), then explain what changes it (Newton's laws), then apply it to friction, circles and gravity.
Describe motion→ 3 Newton's Laws→ Momentum & friction→ Circular & gravity
Anchor ideaA force is a push or pull. Newton's genius was realising that no force is needed to keep moving — only to change motion (speed up, slow down, or turn). That flips everyday intuition and is the root of almost every exam trap here.

📉1 · Kinematics

What
Kinematics describes motion without asking why — using distance, displacement, speed, velocity and acceleration.
Why two words for each
Because direction matters. Walk 3 m east then 3 m west: distance = 6 m but displacement = 0. The scalar/vector pair keeps "how much ground" separate from "how far from start".
How
Acceleration = change in velocity ÷ time. For uniform acceleration, the three equations of motion apply: v = u + at, s = ut + ½at², v² = u² + 2as.
Where
Braking distance, free fall, projectile range, vehicle speed problems.
Exam
"Which equation has no time?" → v² = u² + 2as. "Displacement can be zero while distance isn't" — true. Retardation = negative acceleration.
Scalar/vector pairsDistance↔displacement, speed↔velocity. The first is a scalar (magnitude), the second a vector (magnitude + direction). Uniform velocity needs constant speed AND unchanging direction — going round a circle at steady speed is not uniform velocity.

🛑2 · Newton's First Law (Inertia)

What
A body stays at rest, or keeps moving in a straight line at constant speed, unless a net external force acts. This property is inertia.
Why
Matter has no "memory" to change its own motion — it simply continues whatever it was doing. Force is the only thing that can alter that state. Heavier bodies resist change more, so mass measures inertia.
How
Three flavours: inertia of rest, of motion, and of direction.
Where
Seatbelts, headrests, dusting a carpet, an athlete overshooting the finish, coins under a card flicked away.
Exam
Bus starts → passengers fall back (inertia of rest). Bus brakes → passengers fall forward (inertia of motion). Mud off a spinning wheel → inertia of direction.
🧺 Beating a carpet

Carpet moves, dust stays put (inertia of rest) → dust separates.

🏃 Overshooting

A runner can't stop instantly at the line — inertia of motion.

🪙 Coin & card

Flick the card; the coin drops straight in — inertia of rest.

🚌 Bus jerks

Start/stop throws you back/forward — inertia.

➡️3 · Newton's Second Law (F = ma)

What
The net force on a body equals the rate of change of its momentum; for constant mass this is F = ma.
Why
The first law says force changes motion; the second law says how much. The same push gives a light trolley a big acceleration and a heavy one a small acceleration — acceleration is force shared out over mass.
How
Momentum p = mv. F = Δp/Δt. Impulse = F×t = Δp — so a small force over a long time can produce the same momentum change as a large force over a short time.
Where
Airbags, crumple zones, catching a ball with give, a fast bowler's follow-through, rocket thrust.
Exam
"F = ma" and "1 N = 1 kg·m/s²". "Why bend knees on landing / move hands back while catching?" → increase time → reduce force (impulse).
Why airbags save livesThe change in momentum in a crash is fixed. An airbag lengthens the stopping time, so from Impulse = FΔt = Δp, the force on the body drops dramatically. Same physics as bending your knees when you jump down.

🔄4 · Third Law & Momentum

What
To every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. The two forces act on different bodies. From it follows conservation of momentum.
Why they don't cancel
Action and reaction are equal and opposite, but because they act on two different objects, they never cancel. You push the ground back; the ground pushes you forward — that's how you walk.
How
With no external force, total momentum is conserved: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂. This explains recoil and rocket motion.
Where
Walking, swimming, rowing, gun recoil, rocket/jet propulsion, a balloon zooming when released.
Exam
"Rocket propulsion → 3rd law." "Gun recoil → conservation of momentum." Trap: action & reaction act on different bodies (not the same one).
EverydayA deflating balloon shoots forward as air rushes back; a swimmer pushes water back to glide forward; a squid jets water to move — all Newton's third law plus momentum conservation.

🪵5 · Friction

What
Friction is the force that opposes relative motion (or the tendency of motion) between surfaces in contact.
Why it exists
No surface is perfectly smooth — microscopic bumps interlock and molecular bonds form at contact points. Breaking these takes force, felt as friction. That's also why it turns into heat.
How
Order of size: static (limiting) > kinetic/sliding > rolling. It's hardest to start motion. Laws: f = µN (proportional to normal force), independent of contact area.
Where
Walking, gripping, brakes, matchsticks, writing — and the enemy in engines, tyres and machinery.
Exam
"Largest friction?" → static (limiting). "Smallest?" → rolling. "Ball bearings convert sliding → rolling friction." Reduce friction: lubricants, bearings, polishing, streamlining. Treads increase grip.

🎡6 · Circular Motion

What
Moving in a circle requires a continuous centripetal (centre-seeking) force, F = mv²/r.
Why a force is needed
By the first law a body naturally goes straight. To keep bending its path into a circle, something must constantly pull it toward the centre — gravity for a satellite, tension for a whirled stone, friction/banking for a car.
How
Centrifugal force is the pseudo (fictitious) outward force you seem to feel — really just your inertia trying to go straight. Cut the string and the stone flies off tangentially, not outward.
Where
Banked roads/railways, washing-machine spin dry, cream separators, satellites, a bucket of water swung overhead.
Exam
"Centripetal = toward centre (real)." "Centrifugal = pseudo-force." "Roads are banked to provide centripetal force." Spin dryer → water leaves tangentially.
Centripetal vs CentrifugalCentripetal is a real, centre-pointing force that causes circular motion. Centrifugal is a pseudo-force felt only in the rotating frame. Examiners swap these constantly.

🌍7 · Gravitation

What
Every mass attracts every other with a force F = G·m₁m₂/r². On Earth this gives objects a weight and an acceleration g ≈ 9.8 m/s².
Why all objects fall together
Gravity pulls harder on heavier objects (more mg), but heavier objects also resist acceleration more (more inertia). The two effects cancel exactly, so g is independent of mass — a feather and a coin fall together in vacuum.
How
Weight W = mg. g is larger at the poles (Earth is flattened) and decreases with altitude and depth (zero at the centre). Escape velocity from Earth ≈ 11.2 km/s.
Where
Weighing scales, tides, satellite orbits, planetary motion, "weightlessness" in the ISS.
Exam
"g ≈ 9.8 m/s²", "G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹", "escape velocity ≈ 11.2 km/s", "weight max at poles, zero at Earth's centre". Astronaut weightlessness = free fall, NOT zero gravity.
Why astronauts floatThere is gravity in orbit (about 90% of surface value). Astronauts float because they and their spacecraft are in continuous free fall around Earth — falling together, so there's no support force (apparent weightlessness).

One hook per recall-worthy fact. Concept → hook → recall question.
The three laws in one line chain
"1 stays, 2 weighs, 3 pays back." Law 1 = stays put (inertia); Law 2 = F=ma (mass matters); Law 3 = pays back (reaction).
Q: Which law is F = ma? → Second.
Inertia direction of a jerk funny
Bus starts → you go back (you were resting). Bus stops → you go forward (you were moving). "Opposite to the bus's change."
Q: Bus brakes suddenly — which way do you fall? → Forward.
Friction order mnemonic
"Start is Strongest" → Static > Kinetic > Rolling. Hardest to get moving; easiest to roll.
Q: Which friction is smallest? → Rolling.
Centripetal = centre-seeking word hook
Centri-PET-al = "PETs you toward the centre" (real force). Centri-FUG-al = "FUGitive fleeing outward" (fake/pseudo).
Q: Which force keeps a satellite in orbit? → Centripetal (gravity).
Equations of motion pattern
"vat, suave-half-at², v²=u²+2as." The 3rd one has no t — use it when time is missing.
Q: No time given — which equation? → v² = u² + 2as.
Impulse saves you why-hook
"More time, less force." Airbags, knees, catching hands — all stretch the stopping time so the force drops (Impulse = FΔt = Δp).
Q: Why bend knees on landing? → Increase time → reduce force.
Mass vs Weight peg
Mass is Me everywhere (constant, kg). Weight is Wobbly (W = mg, changes with g, in newtons).
Q: What is zero at Earth's centre — mass or weight? → Weight.
g is bigger at the poles visual
Earth is squashed → poles are closer to the centre → stronger g → you weigh most at the poles. "Polar bears weigh more."
Q: Where is weight maximum on Earth? → Poles.
Escape velocity number peg
11.2 km/s to escape Earth. "Eleven-point-two, break through." G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹; g = 9.8.
Q: Escape velocity from Earth? → 11.2 km/s.
Action–reaction bodies trap-buster
"Two forces, two bodies." They're equal and opposite but on DIFFERENT objects, so they never cancel — that's why you can walk.
Q: Do action and reaction cancel? → No (different bodies).

⚠️Exam Traps & Confusion Pairs

Each has been set in real papers. Read the reality line and it dies.
Trap 1 · Direction of the jerkBus starts → passengers fall backward (inertia of rest). Bus stops → fall forward (inertia of motion). Options often reverse these.
Trap 2 · Action & reaction don't cancelThey are equal and opposite but act on different bodies, so they never cancel. "They cancel each other" is false.
Trap 3 · Mass vs WeightMass (kg) is constant; weight (N) changes with g. On the Moon your weight is ~1/6, but your mass is unchanged.
Trap 4 · Weightlessness ≠ no gravityAstronauts float because they are in free fall, not because gravity is absent. Orbital gravity is ~90% of surface gravity.
Trap 5 · Centrifugal is not realCentrifugal force is a pseudo-force. The real force in circular motion is centripetal (toward the centre).
Trap 6 · Stone released from a slingIt flies off tangentially (straight line), NOT radially outward. This proves there's no real outward force.
Trap 7 · Friction orderStatic (limiting) > kinetic > rolling. "Rolling friction is largest" is false — it's the smallest.
Trap 8 · Treads increase frictionTyre treads and grooved soles increase grip (friction) for safety. Only lubricants, bearings and polishing reduce friction.
Trap 9 · Uniform speed ≠ uniform velocityGoing round a circle at steady speed is accelerated motion, because the direction (hence velocity) keeps changing.
Trap 10 · g is independent of massIn vacuum, all bodies fall with the same g regardless of mass. "Heavier objects fall faster" is only true when air resistance matters.
Trap 11 · Which equation has no time?v² = u² + 2as. Don't reach for v = u + at when time isn't given or asked.
Trap 12 · g at poles vs equatorg is greater at the poles (Earth flattened) and least at the equator — so weight is maximum at the poles.
Assertion–ReasonA: A karate expert breaks a slab quickly. R: A short impact time produces a large force (impulse). → Both true, R explains A.
Assertion–ReasonA: A gun recoils when fired. R: Momentum is conserved and the system starts from rest. → Both true, R explains A.
