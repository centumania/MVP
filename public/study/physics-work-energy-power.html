🌐The Big Picture

Three linked ideas: doing work transfers energy; power is how fast you do it.
Force moves a body = Work→ Capacity to work = Energy→ Work per second = Power
Anchor ideaIn everyday speech "work" means effort; in physics it means a force actually moving something. Push a wall all day and — physically — you've done zero work. Energy is simply "stored ability to do work", and it only ever changes form, never vanishes.

🏋️1 · Work

What
Work is done when a force produces displacement. W = F·s·cosθ, where θ is the angle between force and motion.
Why the cosθ
Only the part of the force along the motion does work. Push a trolley straight ahead → full effect. Push at an angle → only the forward component counts. Push straight down while it rolls forward → nothing counts (θ = 90°).
How
Positive work (θ < 90°, force helps motion), negative work (θ > 90°, e.g. friction), zero work (θ = 90° or no displacement).
Where
Lifting a load (positive), friction stopping a car (negative), carrying a bag on level ground (zero).
Exam
"Coolie carrying load on level road → work against gravity = 0." "Work by friction = negative." "Push a wall → zero work." Unit: joule = N·m.
Three zero-work cases(1) No displacement (pushing a wall). (2) Force ⟂ motion (carrying a load horizontally; a satellite in orbit). (3) No force. Any of these → W = 0.

🔋2 · Forms of Energy

What
Energy comes in many interchangeable forms: mechanical, heat, light, sound, chemical, electrical and nuclear.
Why forms matter
Every device is really an energy converter. Knowing the input and output forms lets you predict what a machine does and where energy is wasted (usually as heat).
How
Mechanical = KE + PE. Chemical in food/fuel/batteries. Nuclear from the nucleus (E = mc²).
Where
Bulb (electrical→light+heat), fan (electrical→mechanical), battery (chemical→electrical), Sun (nuclear→light+heat).
Exam
"Energy in food/fuel?" → chemical. "Sun's energy?" → nuclear fusion. "Bulb converts?" → electrical to light + heat. Loudspeaker → electrical to sound.

🏃3 · Kinetic Energy

What
The energy a body has because it is moving: KE = ½mv².
Why v² (not v)
To speed a body up you must keep doing work against its growing inertia — the work needed rises with the square of speed. That's why a car at 60 km/h has 4× the KE (and roughly 4× the braking distance) of one at 30 km/h.
How
Work–energy theorem: the net work done on a body equals its change in KE (W = ΔKE).
Where
Moving vehicles, wind turbines, flowing water in hydro plants, a bullet, a hammer strike.
Exam
"KE = ½mv²." "Double the speed → 4× KE." "Work–energy theorem: W = ΔKE." KE is a scalar in joules.
Why speeding is dangerousBecause KE ∝ v², a small rise in speed causes a large rise in energy that must be dissipated to stop. Going from 40 to 80 km/h quadruples the kinetic energy and roughly the stopping distance.

⛰️4 · Potential Energy

What
Stored energy due to position or state. Gravitational PE = mgh; elastic PE in a stretched spring or bow.
Why
Lifting a body against gravity stores the work you did — release it and gravity gives that energy back as motion. A stretched spring stores the work done deforming it.
How
PE ↔ KE continuously: a falling body converts PE to KE; a thrown ball converts KE to PE at the top.
Where
Water behind a dam, a drawn bow/catapult, a raised hammer, a wound clock spring, roller-coaster crest.
Exam
"PE = mgh." "Stretched bow → elastic PE." "Water in a dam → gravitational PE (→ electrical in a turbine)."

♻️5 · Conservation of Energy

What
Energy can be neither created nor destroyed — only transformed from one form to another. The total stays constant.
Why it looks like energy "disappears"
Energy never vanishes; it usually turns into heat (via friction) that spreads out and is hard to see. Account for that heat and the books always balance.
How
In a swinging pendulum: PE max at the ends (KE zero) ↔ KE max at the bottom (PE min). PE + KE = constant (ignoring friction).
Where
Pendulums, roller coasters, free fall, hydroelectricity, every energy-conversion device.
Exam
"Energy is neither created nor destroyed." Pendulum: KE max at the mean position, PE max at the extremes. Free fall: PE → KE.
Pendulum: energy shuttles between PE (ends) and KE (bottom); total is constant.

⏱️6 · Power

What
Power is the rate of doing work (or transferring energy): P = W/t. Also P = F·v.
Why it's different from work
Two people who lift the same load up the same stairs do the same work — but the one who does it faster has more power. Power is about speed of energy transfer, not amount.
How
Unit watt (1 W = 1 J/s). 1 hp ≈ 746 W. Energy billed in kWh: 1 kWh = 3.6×10⁶ J.
Where
Engine/motor ratings (kW, hp), electricity bills (kWh), appliance power (100 W bulb).
Exam
"Power unit = watt." "1 hp ≈ 746 W." "kWh is a unit of ENERGY, not power." P = W/t = F·v.
The classic kWh trapkilowatt (kW) is power; kilowatt-hour (kWh) is energy (power × time). The watt is power; the joule and kWh are energy. Examiners love mixing these.

⚙️7 · Efficiency & Machines

What
Efficiency = useful output energy ÷ input energy × 100%. A simple machine makes work easier by giving a mechanical advantage.
Why never 100%
Every real machine loses some energy to friction and heat, so output is always less than input. This also makes perpetual motion impossible.
How
Simple machines: lever, pulley, inclined plane, wheel-and-axle, screw, wedge. Mechanical advantage MA = load ÷ effort. They multiply force or change its direction — but never create energy.
Where
Crowbar, scissors, seesaw, ramp, staircase, bottle opener, pulleys on a crane.
Exam
"Efficiency < 100% always." "MA = load/effort." "A machine multiplies force, not energy." Six simple machines list.
EverydayA ramp (inclined plane) lets you raise a heavy drum with less force — but over a longer distance, so the work is the same. That's the deal with every machine: easier force, never less energy.

🧠Memory Engine

One hook per recall-worthy fact. Concept → hook → recall question.
Work needs movement funny
"No move, no work." Push a wall till you sweat — physics says you did zero work. Displacement is the boss.
Q: Work done pushing an immovable wall? → Zero.
Three formulas, one unit peg
Work, KE and PE are all in joules: "Fs, half-em-vee-squared, em-gee-aitch." Power is the odd one out → watt.
Q: Unit of kinetic energy? → Joule.
KE grows with v² why-hook
"Double speed, quadruple the danger." KE = ½mv² — the v is squared, so 2× speed = 4× energy.
Q: Triple the speed → KE ×? → 9.
Pendulum energy visual
"Ends = PE, Bottom = KE." At the swing's ends it pauses (all potential); at the bottom it races (all kinetic).
Q: Where is KE max in a pendulum? → Lowest point.
Power = work per second peg
"Same work, faster = more power." P = W/t. The watt is Work-per-Time.
Q: 600 J in 3 s → power? → 200 W.
Horsepower number number peg
1 hp ≈ 746 W. "Seven-forty-six horses." Roughly ¾ of a kilowatt.
Q: 1 horsepower ≈ ? watts → 746 W.
kWh is ENERGY trap-buster
"Watt = power, watt-hour = energy." The extra 'hour' (×time) turns power into energy. 1 kWh = 3.6×10⁶ J.
Q: Is kWh power or energy? → Energy.
Energy is never lost story
Energy is like money you can only exchange, never burn — it hides as heat when it seems to vanish. Conservation always balances the books.
Q: Can energy be destroyed? → No, only transformed.
Six simple machines acronym
"Lever, Pulley, Incline, Wheel, Screw, Wedge" → think a crane using them all. MA = load/effort.
Q: Name three simple machines. → Lever, pulley, inclined plane…
Machines cheat force, not energy why-hook
A ramp gives less force but more distance — the work stays the same. "Easier push, longer road." No machine beats 100%.
Q: Can a machine's efficiency exceed 100%? → Never.

⚠️Exam Traps & Confusion Pairs

Each has been set in real papers. Read the reality line and it dies.
Trap 1 · Effort ≠ workStraining hard with no displacement (pushing a wall, holding a weight still) does zero physical work. Work needs movement.
Trap 2 · Carrying on level groundWork done against gravity while carrying a load on a level road is zero (force up ⟂ motion horizontal).
Trap 3 · Friction does negative workFriction opposes motion (θ = 180°), so its work is negative, not positive.
Trap 4 · KE depends on v², not vDouble the speed → four times the KE (and roughly 4× braking distance). Not double.
Trap 5 · Watt vs kWhWatt is power; kilowatt-hour is energy. "The watt is a unit of energy" is false.
Trap 6 · kW vs kWhkW (power) × hours = kWh (energy). A "unit" of electricity billed to you is 1 kWh, an energy unit.
Trap 7 · No machine over 100%Efficiency is always below 100% because of friction/heat. Perpetual-motion machines are impossible.
Trap 8 · Machines don't create energyA lever or pulley multiplies force (or changes direction) but output energy ≤ input. It never reduces the total work.
Trap 9 · Work & energy are scalarsWork, energy and power are all scalars (no direction). "Energy is a vector" is false.
Trap 10 · Energy is never lostIn free fall or a pendulum, energy is not lost — PE simply converts to KE. Any "loss" is heat from friction.
Trap 11 · Satellite & gravityGravity does zero work on a satellite in a circular orbit (force ⟂ motion), even though it holds it in orbit.
Trap 12 · Sun's energy sourceThe Sun runs on nuclear fusion, not chemical burning or combustion.
Assertion–ReasonA: A body in uniform circular motion has constant KE. R: Its speed is constant. → Both true, R explains A.
Assertion–ReasonA: kWh and joule both measure energy. R: Both equal power multiplied by time. → Both true, R explains A.
